📘 09 — Entra ID B2B & B2C (External & Customer Identity)
A simple → deep, Mahabharata‑aligned explanation of how Entra ID handles external users (B2B) and customer identities (B2C).

🏹 **Mahabharata Analogy —
“The Kingdom Gates: Welcoming Allies, Managing Foreigners, Protecting Citizens.”**
In the Mahabharata:

Allies from Panchala, Matsya, and Dwaraka fought beside the Pandavas.
These warriors did not belong to Hastinapura, but were still granted rightful entry into the war council.
They were given:

Valid identification,
Temporary privileges,
Limited access to sacred zones,
And were monitored for loyalty.



Similarly:

B2B = allowing external warriors from allied kingdoms into the royal war tent
B2C = providing citizenship services for lakhs of villagers who interact with the kingdom

This is exactly what Microsoft Entra External ID and Entra ID B2C are designed for.
Microsoft Learn describes these as the way organizations enable external collaboration and customer identity management.
 [learn.microsoft.com]

🔐 1. What Are B2B & B2C Identities? (Simple Definition)
✔ B2B (Business-to-Business)
External users (partners, vendors, contractors) accessing your organization's apps/resources.
✔ B2C (Business-to-Consumer) / Customer Identity
Customer-facing identity platform for apps serving millions of consumers.
Microsoft Entra documentation highlights B2B external collaboration and customer identity (B2C) as core parts of the identity platform.
 [learn.microsoft.com]

🧭 2. Why B2B & B2C Exist (Hastinapur Logic)





























Ancient NeedModern PurposeAllow allies into the battlefieldAllow partners to collaborateGrant temporary safe accessGuest user access with policiesKeep civilians separate from warriorsKeep customer identities separate from corporate usersMaintain strict bordersConditional Access & governanceVerify allies by their own kingsExternal users authenticate with their home IdP

🛡 3. ENTRA ID B2B (Guest Access)
“Invite an ally warrior to fight in your army — without making them part of your kingdom.”
Microsoft Learn states Entra ID B2B allows external users to authenticate using their home organization’s identity provider and access the host’s resources.
 [learn.microsoft.com]
✔ B2B enables:

Guest access into Entra ID
External collaboration across tenants
Partner access to apps
Delegated authentication
Conditional Access enforcement
MFA enforcement (your tenant or theirs)

✔ B2B users authenticate using:

Their own Microsoft Entra tenant
Google / GitHub / SAML / federated IdP
Social or third-party identity providers

✔ B2B user object appears as:
user_type = "Guest"
